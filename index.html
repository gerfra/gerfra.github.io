Welcome

Hello and welcome, on this page you will find my public repositories, which I have decided to share with you. You will find a bit of everything, Javascript, Python, C++ etc... Many of these repositories were written to solve my personal needs, during my free time, such as DxO-Batch-Image which was born from my curiosity and the need to massively process RAW images without using the official program GUI. Or Temperature-Hardware-LibreHardwareMonitorLib which, on the other hand, was extracted from a work project of mine where, among many specifications, I had to monitor hardware components. About me...

Hi curious one, if you've made it this far, then you want to know something about me! My name is Francesco or Gerry, I'm a software developer. I started writing code back in the 80s, like all the kids of that era, I was very impressed by those first computers that became part of the furniture at home. During these long and beautiful years, I had the opportunity to develop many things in various languages, both client-side and server-side, and in recent years, I've also moved into both front-end and back-end applications. I couldn't say which language interested me the most, but back then, the amount of individual effort was much greater. Today, everything is much more enjoyable, even though the proliferation of frameworks and high-level languages is not, in my opinion, a great and beautiful evolution. I'm not a fan of any particular language. In recent years, there's been a lot of talk about how AI will replace all of us in everything, but I still find this to be a pipe dream. In fact, I think it's impractical since the market needs consumers, which means humans will have to do something, right? So, personally, I see AI as valuable support for my thinking brain for all those tedious and repetitive operations that an AI can do, but also a much faster and more precise method to access knowledge when I have gaps in my developer skills! I hope they can even succeed in solving and curing the diseases that afflict humanity. Today, I wrote something "human-readable," and I'm proud of myself! As you know, we're not really very social people... So, now I'll leave you to your business. I hope you found what you discovered about me interesting; otherwise, friends as before!
